Module 1

Understanding Platform Icons

📅 Published June 2025 📂 Platform: Keytelemetrics 👤 Audience: All Team Members ⚙ Version 1.0

Icons are used throughout the Keytelemetrics platform to communicate asset status, alert priority, and available actions — often without displaying any text. Understanding what each icon represents is fundamental to using the platform effectively.

This module covers two categories: descriptive icons (which tell you the current state of something) and functional icons (which you can click to perform an action).

Note: Screenshots in your training environment may differ slightly depending on your assigned client profile and browser settings. All icons described below apply to the standard Keytelemetrics interface.

1. Descriptive Icons

Descriptive icons cannot be clicked — they display the current state of an asset or alert. They appear on the map, in the asset list, and in trip history views.

1.1 Asset Status Icons

These icons appear next to each vehicle in the asset list and on the live map. They update in real time as the vehicle state changes.

IconDescription
Asset Status
PARKED Asset is parked. Ignition is OFF. The vehicle is stationary and the engine is not running. No trip is in progress.
MOVING Asset is moving. Ignition is ON and speed is greater than zero. A trip is actively in progress.
IDLING Asset is stopped or idling. Ignition is ON but speed is zero. The engine is running but the vehicle is not moving. May indicate driver waiting time or unauthorised idling.
Tip: Idling events consume fuel without productive movement. Excessive idling will appear in driver behaviour reports and may trigger alerts depending on your fleet configuration.

1.2 Alert Priority Icons

Alert priority icons appear in the Alerts panel, trip replay, and event lists. They indicate how urgently an alert requires attention.

IconDescription
Alert Priority
LOW Low priority alert or informational event. Requires monitoring but no immediate action. Examples: geofence entry, scheduled maintenance reminder.
MEDIUM Medium priority alert. Requires attention within a reasonable timeframe. Examples: speeding, harsh braking, extended idling.
HIGH High priority alert. Requires immediate attention. Examples: panic button activation, tampering detection, extreme speeding.

1.3 Trip Rating Icon

The trip rating icon appears in Trip History and Trip Details views. It gives a quick visual summary of driver behaviour quality for that trip.

IconDescription
Trip Rating
66–100% Good trip rating (66–100%). Driver behaviour was acceptable throughout the trip. Few or no events triggered.
33–66% Average trip rating (33–66%). Some driver behaviour events were recorded. Review the trip detail for specifics.
0–33% Poor trip rating (0–33%). Significant driver behaviour events recorded. This trip should be reviewed and discussed with the driver.

2. Functional Icons

Functional icons can be clicked to perform an action. They appear on maps, in menus, and throughout the administration and reporting sections. Hovering over a functional icon will usually reveal a tooltip describing what it does.

2.1 Maps and General

IconDescription
Maps and General
CENTRECentre the map on all assets within the selected client.
FULLSCROpen a fullscreen map showing the asset or assets in view.
LAYERSChange the map layer — toggle between satellite, hybrid, or standard street map view.
+ / −Zoom in and zoom out controls on the map view.
ROUTETop button: Route creation tool. Bottom button: Zone creation tool.
EDITTop button: Edit an existing zone. Bottom button: Delete an existing zone.
OPTIONSOptions menu — reveals additional actions for the selected item.
FILTERFilter the asset or alert list by criteria such as status, group, or date range.
SEARCHSearch for a specific asset, zone, or record by name or identifier.
POP-OUTOpen the current data in a new pop-up window for side-by-side viewing.
MEASUREMeasurement tool — draw a line on the map to measure distance between two points.
ZONESToggle the display of zones and routes on the live map.
INFOPoint information — click a location on the map to view its address details.

2.2 Menu Navigation Icons

These icons form the left-hand navigation menu. The active section icon will appear in red. Clicking any icon takes you directly to that section of the platform.

IconSection
Menu Navigation
OVERVIEWLive map and asset status panel. This is the default home screen.
ALERTSView and manage triggered alerts for your fleet.
TRIPTrip Replay — replay historical trips for any asset, including events and telemetry.
REPORTReporting — access scheduled and on-demand fleet reports.
ADMINAdministration — manage assets, users, alerts, zones, and device configuration.
CLIENTChange Client — switch between different client accounts you have access to.
LOGOUTLogout — end your current session securely.
DASHDashboards — access visual dashboard widgets and KPI displays.
NOTIFNotifications — view system and account notifications.
PROFILEProfile — view and update your user account settings.

2.3 Event Icons

Event icons appear in the Events and Alerts sections. Clicking them opens associated media captured at the time of the event.

IconDescription
Event Media Icons
PHOTOPhoto event. Clicking this icon opens the photograph captured at the time of the event in a separate pop-up window.
VIDEOVideo event. Clicking this icon opens the video clip or clips captured at the time of the event in a separate pop-up window.

2.4 Administration Icons

These icons appear throughout the Administration section and are used to manage assets, devices, users, zones, and alert configurations.

IconDescription
Administration
VENDORVendor level — navigate to vendor-level settings and details.
CLIENTClient / Home — return to the client home level in the administration hierarchy.
+ ADDAdd new item — create a new asset, user, alert rule, zone, or other record.
REFRESHRefresh list — reload the current list to reflect the latest data.
FILTERFilter list — narrow down the displayed records by applying filter criteria.
COLUMNSChoose columns — customise which columns are displayed in the current list view.
UPGo up a level — navigate one level up in the administration hierarchy.
SEARCHSearch — find a specific record within the current administration list.
STATEDelete / Change state — activate, deactivate, suspend, or delete the selected item.
CLOSEClose item — discard changes and close the current record without saving.
EDITEdit item — open the selected record for editing.
